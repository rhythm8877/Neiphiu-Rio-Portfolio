Biography

Neiphiu Rio, born on 11 November 1950 in Tuophema village, Kohima district, is a prominent political leader from North East India and the longest-serving Chief Minister of Nagaland. His legacy reflects unwavering dedication to public welfare, dynamic leadership, and a vision for lasting peace and inclusive growth across the region.

A graduate of Kohima Arts College, Neiphiu Rio values education as a tool for empowerment. Beyond politics, he is admired for nurturing the state's rich cultural identity through music, indigenous arts, and sports. His efforts continue to shape a confident, progressive Nagaland with deep roots in tradition.

Since entering politics in 1989, he has steadily risen to become a transformative figure in Nagaland's development journey. Under his leadership, the state has seen notable advancements in rural upliftment, youth engagement, skill development, and cultural preservation, earning widespread respect from diverse communities.

Achievements

Mother Teresa Millennium Award

Received the prestigious Mother Teresa Millennium Award in 2007 for his contributions to society.

Nagaland Skill Mission

Launched the Nagaland Skill Mission in 2025 to empower youth with essential skills for the future.

Centre for Excellence in Music & Arts

Established a world-class center to nurture Nagaland's rich musical and artistic heritage.

Nagaland Solar Mission

Initiated the Nagaland Solar Mission in 2025 to promote sustainable energy solutions across the state.

Peace-builder & Youth Advocate

Dedicated to fostering peace and empowering the youth of Nagaland through various initiatives.

5-term Chief Minister

Served as Chief Minister of Nagaland for five terms (2003-2025), making him the longest-serving CM in the state's history.
